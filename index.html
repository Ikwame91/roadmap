When You Stall — Recovery Protocol

You will miss a Saturday. Maybe two. Maybe a whole month. This is not failure — it's expected. Here's how you come back:

1. Don't restart. Open your roadmap. Find the last thing you checked off. Start from the next one. You didn't lose your progress.

2. Shrink the session. If 3 hours feels impossible, do 45 minutes. The goal is to break the stall, not to catch up in one day.

3. Re-explain the last thing you learned. Say it out loud. If you still can, you're closer than you think. If you can't, that's your next session — not a failure, a starting point.

4. Text someone. Send a DevCongress friend or a peer your 3 sentences. Accountability survives isolation. Silence kills momentum.

Study Rules
01
One source per topic at a time
Don't watch 3 YouTube videos on the same thing. Pick one source, go deep, move on. Tutorial-hopping feels productive but teaches nothing.
02
Never read without a pen
Physical notebook or Obsidian/Notion — doesn't matter. But every Friday session produces written notes in your own words. Not copy-pasted. Rewritten.
03
The 20-minute rule
If you're stuck for more than 20 minutes, write down exactly what you're stuck on in one sentence. Then ask for help. The sentence is the important part — it forces you to articulate the gap.
04
Don't binge on weekdays
You're drained by 8pm. The 30-minute weekday window is for review only. Re-read your notebook. Draw one diagram. Do one LeetCode. That's it. New learning on weekdays will frustrate you and erode Saturday motivation.
05
Build ugly first
Your first Task API will be messy. Your first SQL queries will be slow. Your HTTP server will crash. This is correct. Clean code comes from understanding what dirty code breaks — not from copying clean patterns you don't understand.
Mindset Shifts
"I already know this" is your biggest enemy
You've built APIs. You've used TypeScript. The temptation in Phase 1 will be to rush. Don't. The goal is not to use TypeScript — it's to explain TypeScript. There's a massive gap between the two. CropScan proved you can build. This roadmap proves you can think.
Learning feels slow when it's working
If you finish a Saturday session and feel like you barely covered anything — that's depth. That's the goal. If you covered 5 topics in 3 hours, you learned none of them.
Your job is not wasted time
Bank of Africa gives you domain knowledge in fintech, data validation, and institutional systems. When you build your capstone, that context is your unfair advantage. No bootcamp graduate has it. Use it.
